Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence include

Thomas Jefferson (Virginia)
Principal author of the Declaration of Independence and later the 3rd U.S. President.
John Adams (Massachusetts)
Strong advocate for independence; became the 2nd U.S. President.
Benjamin Franklin (Pennsylvania)
Renowned inventor, diplomat, and elder statesman of the American Revolution.
John Hancock (Massachusetts)
President of the Continental Congress; famous for his large, bold signature on the Declaration.
Samuel Adams (Massachusetts)
Revolutionary leader and organizer of the Boston Tea Party.
Richard Henry Lee (Virginia)
Introduced the resolution calling for independence from Britain in the Continental Congress.
George Wythe (Virginia)
Mentor to Thomas Jefferson and the first American law professor.
Charles Carroll of Carrollton (Maryland)
The only Catholic signer and one of the wealthiest men in the colonies.
Thomas Nelson Jr. (Virginia)
Later commanded Virginia troops and signed the Declaration at great personal cost.

These influential figures were among the fifty-six men who risked their lives, fortunes, and reputations by signing the Declaration of Independence.
